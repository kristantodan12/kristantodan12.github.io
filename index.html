Daniel Kristanto

I work as a post-doc at the Psychological Methods and Statistics Lab, Department of Psychology, University of Oldenburg (link). I am currently working within the METAREP programme (link), focusing on methods (e.g. multiverse analysis (link)) to address the replication challenge in cognitive neuroscience research.

I received my Ph.D. from the Department of Physics, Hong Kong Baptist University, under the supervision of Professor Changsong Zhou. My research implemented data-driven approaches to understand brain-behaviour relationships. Prior to my Ph.D., I obtained Master's and Bachelor's degrees in Engineering from Sirindhorn International Institute of Technology, Thailand and Universitas Gadjah Mada, Indonesia, respectively.

My research interests include researching research, method development and method implementation in the field of cognitive neuroscience. Specifically, I use approaches from machine learning and statistical methods to investigate the uniqueness of each individual brain and its relationship to behaviour and disease.
