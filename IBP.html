The Individual Brain Project

Optimizing Individuality Approaches in Neuroimaging Research - A Knowledge Graph-Based AI Development

Overview

The Individual Brain Project is designed to address key challenges in precision neuroimaging research trend: research fragmentation and increased researcher degrees of freedom. These challenges highlight the critical need for a systematic, integrated framework, which the Individual Brain Project provides as the proposed solution. At the core of the project is the development of a Curated Knowledge Graph (KG-IBP), where nodes represent detailed information about studies and methods, and edges capture rich relationships including similarities and benchmark comparisons. The KG-IBP is meticulously built using systematic review and curation (WP1) and comparative benchmarking (WP2). This structured knowledge base subsequently guides novel method development (WP3) and enables the creation of the project's key outputs: an open 'Individual Brain Toolbox' (WP4) and context-aware AI tools like method recommenders (WP5). This integrated ecosystem aims to foster cohesive research efforts and accelerate robust, impactful progress in precision neuroimaging.
